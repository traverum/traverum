# Implementation Guidance for Product Team

This section is not part of the customer-facing Terms and Conditions. It is intended as guidance for the Traverum product and engineering team regarding how and where to implement Terms acceptance in the booking flow.

---

## 1. Where in the Booking Flow the Terms Should Be Shown

The Terms acceptance must appear on the **Checkout page**, immediately before the Customer submits the form — i.e., directly above the "Book Now" or "Send Request" button in the `CheckoutForm` component (`apps/widget/src/components/CheckoutForm.tsx`).

This is the last step before either:
- **Session-based bookings:** the Customer is redirected to the Stripe Payment Link, or
- **Request-based bookings:** the Reservation request is submitted to the Experience Provider.

This is the legally decisive moment: the Customer is providing personal data and consenting to a contract being formed (immediately or upon Provider acceptance + payment).

**Do NOT place Terms acceptance on:**
- The experience browsing page (too early, not a contractual step)
- The Stripe payment page (Traverum has no control over the Stripe-hosted page)
- The confirmation page (too late, agreement already formed)

---

## 2. Checkbox Placement and Design

### Recommended approach: Passive acceptance with a prominent link (no checkbox)

The current industry standard (used by Airbnb, GetYourGuide, Viator, and Booking.com) is **passive acceptance** — a clear text line near the submit button stating that by proceeding, the user agrees to the Terms. This approach is legally valid under EU law when:
- The Terms are clearly referenced and easily accessible (linked)
- The text is visible and not hidden
- The action (clicking the button) is unambiguous

### Recommended wording (session-based, instant booking):

> By clicking "Book Now", you agree to Traverum's [Terms & Conditions](/terms) and [Privacy Policy](/privacy), and acknowledge that this experience is provided and operated by **{Provider Name}**, not by Traverum.

### Recommended wording (request-based booking):

> By clicking "Send Request", you agree to Traverum's [Terms & Conditions](/terms) and [Privacy Policy](/privacy), and acknowledge that this experience is provided and operated by **{Provider Name}**, not by Traverum. You won't be charged until the provider accepts and you complete payment.

### Alternative approach: Active acceptance with checkbox

If a stricter approach is desired (e.g., for certain jurisdictions or to reduce dispute risk), add a mandatory checkbox:

```
[ ] I agree to Traverum's Terms & Conditions and Privacy Policy,
    and acknowledge that this experience is provided by {Provider Name}.
```

The submit button should be disabled until the checkbox is checked.

### Key design principles:
- The text must be **above** or **immediately adjacent to** the submit button
- Links to Terms and Privacy Policy must open in a new tab
- The Provider name must be visible and specific (not generic)
- Font size should be at minimum 12px (not tiny/hidden)
- Text colour should have sufficient contrast (WCAG AA minimum)

## 3. Provider Name Visibility Before Acceptance

**Yes — the Experience Provider's name must appear before the Customer agrees to the Terms.**

This is critical for the legal positioning of Traverum as an intermediary. The Customer must know *who* they are contracting with *before* they consent. This is required by:

- **EU Consumer Rights Directive (2011/83/EU), Article 6(1)(b):** The trader's identity must be disclosed before the consumer is bound by a contract.
- **EU Package Travel Directive (2015/2302):** Requires clear identification of the organiser (the Experience Provider, in our case).
- **Airbnb, GetYourGuide, Viator precedent:** All display the provider/host name prominently before booking.

The Provider name should appear:
1. In the booking summary on the checkout page (already present in the experience title area)
2. In the Terms acceptance text itself (as shown in the recommended wording above)
3. In the booking confirmation email (already implemented)

## 5. Best Practices from Major OTAs

| Platform | Approach | Details |
|----------|----------|---------|
| **Airbnb** | Passive acceptance | "By selecting the button below, I agree to the Host's House Rules, Ground rules for guests, Airbnb's Rebooking and Refund Policy, and that Airbnb can charge my payment method..." Clearly identifies the Host. No checkbox. |
| **GetYourGuide** | Passive acceptance | "By completing this booking you agree to the General Terms and Conditions, the activity provider's terms and the Privacy Policy." Provider name shown in the booking summary. |
| **Viator** | Passive acceptance | "By clicking 'Reserve Now', you agree to our Terms & Conditions and Privacy Policy." Provider name shown in the experience details. |
| **Booking.com** | Passive acceptance + highlight | "By clicking 'Reserve', you agree to the booking conditions..." Important conditions (cancellation policy) are displayed prominently with visual emphasis. |

### Key takeaways:
1. **All major OTAs use passive acceptance** (no checkbox) — it's the industry standard
2. **All clearly identify the provider/host** before the point of acceptance
3. **All link to full Terms** (opening in a new tab)
4. **Cancellation policy is surfaced separately** from the general Terms, with visual emphasis
5. **None bury the Terms** — the text is visible and near the action button
6. **All differentiate the intermediary role** in the Terms themselves, even if they don't repeat it on the checkout page

## 6. Additional Recommendations

### 6.1. Terms Page Route

Create a static page at `/terms` (e.g., `apps/widget/src/app/terms/page.tsx`) that renders the Terms and Conditions. This page should:
- Be publicly accessible (no authentication required)
- Have a clean, readable layout
- Show the last-updated date prominently
- Be indexed by search engines

Similarly, create a `/privacy` page for the Privacy Policy.

### 6.2. Version Control and Audit Trail

Consider logging the Terms version the Customer agreed to at the time of booking. Store the Terms version identifier (e.g., a date or version number) in the `reservations` table alongside `terms_accepted_at` (timestamp). This creates an audit trail for regulatory compliance.

### 6.3. Cancellation Policy Display

The Experience's cancellation policy is already displayed on the experience detail page. Ensure it is also visible on the checkout page (above the Terms acceptance text) so the Customer sees it before committing. This is consistent with Booking.com's approach and reduces dispute frequency.

### 6.4. Email Footer

Include a brief reference to the Terms in all transactional emails:
> "This booking is subject to Traverum's Terms & Conditions. The experience is provided by {Provider Name}."

### 6.5. Embed Widget Considerations

When the booking flow is accessed via an embedded widget on a Distribution Partner's website, the checkout flow opens in a new tab on `book.veyond.eu`. The Terms acceptance occurs in this new tab, within Traverum's domain — ensuring clear attribution and avoiding any confusion with the hotel's own terms.
