## TRAVERUM Vision: The Core Problem We Solve

### The Fundamental Gap

**Experience suppliers** (tour operators, activity providers, guides) create amazing local experiences but face a critical problem:
- Customer acquisition is expensive (marketing, advertising, SEO)
- Being on hotels channels is great but without technical solutions like Traverum it is very time consuming to keep these connections with hotels

**Hotels** face a different problem:
- Hotels recommend local experiences manually on reception, without commission
- Travelers book accommodation, but want curated local experiences. The demand is high.
- They're missing revenue opportunities and becoming less relevant as destinations evolve beyond "just a place to sleep"

### The White-Label Solution

Traverum creates a **direct bridge**: Hotels become experience curators for their local suppliers by selling local experiences via their channels. Automatically in website widget, confrimation emails and qr-codes.

**For Experience Suppliers:**
- **Risk-free marketing** - Pay commission only when customers book (no upfront marketing spend)
- **Multiple customer sources** - Reach travelers through many hotel partners, reducing dependency on any single channel
- **Instant distribution** - Get access to hotels' existing customer bases without building relationships for years
- **Professional presentation** - Appear on professional hotel websites (not competing marketplaces)

**For Hotels:**
- **New revenue stream** - Earn commissions on experiences without inventory or operational burden
- **Increased site relevance** - Become a destination hub, not just a room provider
- **Better traveler experience** - Offer guests one-stop shopping for accommodation + activities
- **Competitive advantage** - Keep travelers on their own website
- **Curation & control** - Choose which local suppliers to feature, maintaining brand quality

### Why This Matters

Hotels and suppliers benefit from each other, but they don't know each other. Traverum is the infrastructure that connects them at scale, enabling a local cross-selling ecosystem that didn't exist before.

**In practice:** A hotel in Finnish Lapland embeds Arctic safari experiences on their website. Guests see the widget as part of the hotel's offering. Safari operators get customers without spending ads. The hotel earns 12% commission on every booking. Everyone wins.

**At scale:** Experience operators reach high value customers. Hotels become distribution channels. Travelers get seamless booking. The system is sustainable because value flows naturally. Suppliers pay commission to reach customers, hotels earn commission from their customer attention.

## Core White-Label Architecture

### Three-Party Ecosystem

**Suppliers** (Experience Operators, Activity Providers, Restaurants, Events)
Create and manage experiences
- Own their products
- Set pricing and availability
- View their own bookings and earnings across all distributions
- Integrate Stripe Connect account for revenue settlement

**Distributors** (Hotels, Resorts, Accommodations)
Embed and sell supplier experiences
- Control which supplier experiences appear on their channels
- Earn commission on each sale
- Brand the experience widget to match their site
- View commission and sales reports

**Customers** (End Travelers)
Book experiences seamlessly
- Shop on the distributor's website (not any OTA platform)
- Complete purchase with one transaction
- Receive confirmation from the distributor's perspective
